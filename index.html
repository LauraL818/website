My name is Laura and I am sports and outdoor enthusiast. Growing up in Colorado, I spent much of my childhood skiing, snowboarding, hiking, fishing, camping, and backpacking. When I wasn't exploring the outdoors I was almost certainly playing or watching a sport. My love for the outdoors and sports has only grown stronger as I have gotten older. Today, I am a diehard Colorado sports fan, but nothing can trump my love for the Nebraska Cornhuskers football program.

I attended the University of Denver where I received a degree in marketing with a minor in finance. After graduation, I moved to Los Angeles to work in entertainment marketing. I worked as an assistant media planner for Universal McCann on the Sony Pictures Entertainment account. During my time at UM, I became the go to person for competitive analysis. My ability to quickly gather, pivot, and draw conclusions from large sets of data helped the team better understand the current trends in the industry. The fast-paced, tight deadline, collaborative nature of the media industry helped me become an efficient team player who can produce quick and accurate work.

Most recently, I graduated from General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program. During the 12 week program, I built four projects using a variety of web-based technologies. Each project was built in a one week sprint and helped to solidify the knowledge we gained during each unit. During the program, I discovered my passion for data visualization. The last project, which was built using the MEAN Stack, allowed me to explore and develop my data visualization skills using tools such as D3.js and Chart.js. Overall, the Web Development Immersive program has given me the strong foundation I need to pursue what I hope will be a long career in web development.

Long Shot is a two player sports trivia game aimed to both challenge and entertain the avid sports fan. The two players take turns answering a variety of sports questions until one player scores three goals and comes out victorious.

Long Shot

Baseball Journal is driven towards enhancing a baseball lover's stadium experience. The app gives users the ability to store memories and photos from stadiums they have been to and to search for basic facts about each stadium.

Baseball Journal

VentCal is an app that is targeted towards people looking for events in their area. Using the Yelp, EventFul, SeatGeek, and SoundCloud APIs, users can search for events in their area and add events that they like to their calendar.

VentCal

Movie Index provides movie data and information in a more dynamic and interactive way than other sites of its kind. The app allows users to search for films, view basic information, and receive a financial breakdown of each film.
